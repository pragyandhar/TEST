SECURITY INCIDENT REPORT

Report ID: SEC-2023-0728-BREACH
Date Filed: August 5, 2023
Filed By: David Chen, Director of IT Security

INCIDENT SUMMARY:
On July 28, 2023, our company experienced a data breach affecting approximately 45,000 customer records. Unauthorized access was detected and stopped within 20 minutes.

TIMELINE OF EVENTS:

July 20, 2023, 9:47 AM:
- Phishing email sent to sarah.martinez@company.com
- Subject: "Urgent: Verify Your Account Access"
- Email appeared to be from IT Support but originated from external domain

July 20, 2023, 10:15 AM:
- Sarah Martinez accessed the phishing link (confirmed by web proxy logs)
- She entered credentials on fake login page
- NO report was filed by Sarah

July 28, 2023, 2:15 PM:
- Automated alert triggered due to unusual database activity
- Michael Roberts (IT Manager) responded immediately

July 28, 2023, 2:35 PM:
- Compromised account disabled
- Malicious IP blocked
- Access stopped

July 28, 2023, 3:00 PM:
- Incident response team assembled
- Forensic analysis initiated

FINDINGS:

1. ROOT CAUSE: Successful credential phishing attack
2. EMPLOYEE INVOLVED: Sarah Martinez failed to report suspicious email and likely clicked the phishing link despite her denial
3. DATA COMPROMISED: 45,000 customer records (names, emails, purchase history) - NO payment data exposed
4. ATTACKER LOCATION: IP traced to Romania; likely VPN/proxy
5. TIME TO DETECTION: 8 days (delay due to Sarah's vacation)

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN:

1. All employee passwords reset immediately
2. Mandatory multi-factor authentication (MFA) enabled company-wide
3. Enhanced email filtering rules deployed
4. Security awareness training scheduled for all staff

DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS:

Sarah Martinez has been placed on administrative leave pending investigation. Her failure to report the phishing attempt violated security policy Section 4.2.

RECOMMENDATIONS:

1. Implement quarterly phishing simulation tests
2. Require MFA for all remote access (not just VPN)
3. Review and strengthen employee security training program
4. Consider implementing zero-trust architecture

- Regulatory agencies: Filed required reports August 12
